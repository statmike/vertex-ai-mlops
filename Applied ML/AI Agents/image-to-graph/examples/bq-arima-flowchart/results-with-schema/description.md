This flowchart depicts a comprehensive time series forecasting and analysis pipeline, specifically representing the architecture of a machine learning system like BigQuery ML's ARIMA_PLUS. The system is designed to ingest raw time series data, clean it, decompose it into underlying patterns and anomalies, and ultimately produce multiple analytical outputs including future forecasts, explainable components, and anomaly detection. Visually, the process flows from left to right and is organized into three clearly defined boundaries demarcated by dashed lines: "Preprocessing," "Modeling," and "Decomposed Time Series," which finally lead to a set of distinct output data stores on the far right.

The diagram utilizes specific shapes and color-coding to distinguish different types of architectural elements. Cylinders represent data storage or data states, including the initial inputs, intermediate residual series (colored grey), the isolated decomposed components, and the final analytical outputs (colored orange or purple). Rectangular blocks represent active processing algorithms or computational steps. Within the modeling and decomposition phases, a distinct color scheme separates different classes of time series behaviors: light green indicates structural, recurring elements (holidays, seasonality, and trends), while light yellow denotes localized, non-recurring anomalies (spikes, dips, and step changes).

The data flow begins on the far left with two primary sources: an "Input Time Series" and an optional "Input Custom Holiday" data store. The input time series enters the "Preprocessing" section, where it undergoes a strict, sequential pipeline of data cleaning operations. The data moves through "Duplicated Timestamp Handling," proceeds to "Data Frequency Inference," advances to "Irregular Timestamp Handling," and concludes with "Missing Data Interpolation." Once the data is normalized and cleaned, it is passed into the central "Modeling" phase.

The "Modeling" phase consists of a cascading sequence of adjustments and decompositions, where each algorithmic step extracts a specific behavioral component and passes the remaining residual data to the next step. First, the data undergoes "Holiday Adjustment" (informed by the Custom Holiday input), which isolates a "Holiday Component" and outputs an intermediate "De-holiday'ed Time Series." This residual series flows into "Spikes & Dips Outlier Cleaning," producing an "Outlier Component" and a "De-outlier'ed Time Series." Next, "Seasonal & Trend Decomposition (STL)" extracts "Multiple Seasonal Components" and yields a "De-seasoned Time Series." The data then moves to "Step Change Adjustment," isolating a "Step Change Component" and leaving a "Step-change Adjusted Time Series." Finally, this series enters "Trend Modeling (distributed auto.ARIMA)," which establishes the "Trend Component" and leaves a final "Residual Time Series." 

The relationships between the isolated components and the final outputs dictate the varied capabilities of the system. The isolated features are collected in the "Decomposed Time Series" section before being routed to specific final outputs. To generate the primary forecast, "Point Forecast" data from the structural components—specifically the Holiday, Multiple Seasonal, and Trend components—are routed to an "Aggregation" operator (represented by a red circle with a cross). This aggregation produces the "Forecasted Time Series + Intervals" output, which is associated with the `ML.FORECAST` function. Simultaneously, all five extracted components (Holiday, Outlier, Seasonal, Step Change, and Trend) are fed directly into a combined output data store representing the `ML.EXPLAIN_FORECAST` function, allowing users to understand the drivers of the model. Furthermore, "Point Forecast + Intervals" from the structural Holiday, Seasonal, and Trend components are combined to generate the "Fitted History Series + Forecasted Series + Intervals" output, powering the `ML.DETECT_ANOMALIES` function. Independent of the component aggregation, the final "Trend Modeling" algorithm directly outputs a separate data store containing "Evaluation Metrics + Model Coefficients," providing model diagnostics via the `ML.ARIMA_EVALUATE` and `ML.ARIMA_COEFFICIENTS` functions.
