This diagram is a detailed flowchart representing an automated time series forecasting and data decomposition pipeline. Based on the specific functions noted in the outputs, it illustrates the underlying architectural process for BigQuery ML's ARIMA_PLUS modeling system. It maps the end-to-end journey of raw time series data, showing how it is cleaned, broken down into its constituent mathematical components, and ultimately reassembled to generate forecasts, explain models, and detect anomalies.

The visual structure of the diagram is organized horizontally into distinct operational phases, primarily delineated by three large, dashed bounding boxes: "Preprocessing," "Modeling," and "Decomposed Time Series." Data inputs enter from the far left, progress sequentially through the preprocessing and modeling phases in the center, and deposit extracted components into the decomposition grouping. The flow culminates on the far right and bottom left in a series of final output repositories, represented as orange and purple cylinders. 

The process begins with two initial data sources: an "Input Time Series" and an optional "Input Custom Holiday" dataset. The raw time series data first enters the "Preprocessing" phase. This phase consists of a linear sequence of data-cleaning steps, represented by blue rectangular process nodes. The data flows top-to-bottom through "Duplicated Timestamp Handling," "Data Frequency Inference," "Irregular Timestamp Handling," and finally "Missing Data Interpolation," ensuring the data is uniformly formatted before modeling begins.

Once preprocessed, the cleaned data enters the "Modeling" phase, which functions as an iterative decomposition loop. The defining characteristic of this phase is how each modeling step extracts a specific mathematical pattern and passes the remaining, adjusted data to the next step. First, the preprocessed data and the "Input Custom Holiday" are fed into "Holiday Adjustment." This process extracts a "Holiday Component," which is stored in the "Decomposed Time Series" section, and produces a "De-holiday'ed Time Series." This intermediate series moves down to "Spikes & Dips Outlier Cleaning," which isolates an "Outlier Component" and passes down a "De-outlier'ed Time Series." Next, "Seasonal & Trend Decomposition (STL)" extracts "Multiple Seasonal Components" and yields a "De-seasoned Time Series." This is followed by "Step Change Adjustment," which pulls out a "Step Change Component" and outputs a "Step-change Adjusted Time Series." Finally, this remaining data undergoes "Trend Modeling (distributed auto.ARIMA)," which extracts the final "Trend Component" and leaves behind a "Residual Time Series."

A critical relationship in the diagram occurs after the decomposition is complete. Four of the extracted elements stored in the "Decomposed Time Series" block—specifically the Holiday, Multiple Seasonal, Step Change, and Trend components—are routed via paths labeled "Point Forecast" into a central "Aggregation" node, depicted as a red circle with a cross. Notably, the "Outlier Component" bypasses this aggregation step. This represents the reassembly of the underlying patterns to create a future prediction.

The pipeline produces four distinct final outputs, categorized by their corresponding machine learning functions. The result of the "Aggregation" node, labeled "Point Forecast + Intervals," generates the "Forecasted Time Series + Intervals" output (associated with the `ML.FORECAST` function). The collective set of components from the decomposition phase feeds directly into an output stack representing the "Seasonal, Trend, Holiday, Outlier, Step Change Components," which is used to interpret the model (`ML.EXPLAIN_FORECAST`). A specific data flow from the components phase also generates the "Fitted History Series + Forecasted Series + Intervals" output, used for identifying irregularities (`ML.DETECT_ANOMALIES`). Finally, returning to the bottom of the modeling phase, the "Trend Modeling" step feeds directly into a distinct output repository for "Evaluation Metrics + Model Coefficients" (`ML.ARIMA_EVALUATE` and `ML.ARIMA_COEFFICIENTS`), which provides the statistical assessment of the model's performance.
