This flowchart illustrates a comprehensive machine learning pipeline for time series forecasting and analysis, representing the underlying architecture of an automated forecasting system, likely Google Cloud's BigQuery ML ARIMA_PLUS. The diagram maps the end-to-end journey of raw time series data through data cleaning, iterative structural decomposition, trend modeling, and the generation of final forecasts, anomaly detection, and evaluation metrics. 

The structure of the diagram is organized into three primary sequential phases, clearly delineated by dashed rectangular boundaries: "Preprocessing," "Modeling," and "Decomposed Time Series." The general data flow moves from the initial inputs on the far left, progresses rightwards through the sequential processing and modeling groups, and culminates in various output databases and models located on the far right and bottom left. Nodes are distinguished by shape, using cylinders to represent data storage or model states, rectangles for processing operations, and a circle for data aggregation.

The process initiates with two primary data sources: the "Input Time Series" and an optional "Input Custom Holiday" dataset. The raw "Input Time Series" feeds directly into the "Preprocessing" group. This phase is a strict linear sequence designed to clean and regularize the data. It begins with "Duplicated Timestamp Handling," passes through "Data Frequency Inference," proceeds to "Irregular Timestamp Handling," and concludes with "Missing Data Interpolation." 

Once the data is preprocessed, it enters the "Modeling" group, which is characterized by an iterative extraction and reduction process. The interpolated data, along with the "Input Custom Holiday" data, first enters the "Holiday Adjustment" node. This node performs two actions: it extracts the "Holiday Component" (sending it to the "Decomposed Time Series" group) and outputs a "De-holiday'ed Time Series." This modified time series serves as the input for the next step, "Spikes & Dips Outlier Cleaning." This pattern repeats throughout the modeling phase. The outlier cleaning step extracts the "Outlier Component" and passes a "De-outlier'ed Time Series" to "Seasonal & Trend Decomposition (STL)." The STL process separates out "Multiple Seasonal Components" and forwards a "De-seasoned Time Series" to "Step Change Adjustment." After extracting the "Step Change Component," the remaining "Step-change Adjusted Time Series" is routed to the final modeling operation: "Trend Modeling (distributed auto.ARIMA)." This final step isolates the "Trend Component" and generates a final "Residual Time Series."

The individual components extracted during the modeling phase—Holiday, Outlier, Multiple Seasonal, Step Change, and Trend—are stored within the "Decomposed Time Series" grouping. A key relationship in the diagram is how these separated elements are recombined to create final predictions. Paths labeled "Point Forecast" flow from the Holiday, Outlier, Seasonal, and Step Change components into a central "Aggregation" node. The "Trend Component" also feeds into this aggregation, but its connection is specifically designated as providing a "Point Forecast + Intervals." The output of this aggregation node yields the "Forecasted Time Series + Intervals," which is associated with the BigQuery function ML.FORECAST.

In addition to the primary forecast, the system produces several other specialized outputs. The entire collection of elements within the "Decomposed Time Series" group is gathered into an output labeled "Seasonal, Trend, Holiday, Outlier, Step Change Components," utilized by the ML.EXPLAIN_FORECAST function to provide interpretability. The "Trend Component" independently supplies data to generate a "Fitted History Series + Forecasted Series + Intervals" output, powering the ML.DETECT_ANOMALIES function. Finally, the "Trend Modeling (distributed auto.ARIMA)" node directly outputs a database of "Evaluation Metrics + Model Coefficients," which corresponds to the ML.ARIMA_EVALUATE and ML.ARIMA_COEFFICIENTS functions, allowing users to assess the model's performance and internal parameters.
